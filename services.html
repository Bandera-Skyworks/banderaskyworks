Energy & Utilities

Powerline Inspections

Our comprehensive powerline inspection services leverage cutting-edge automated flight technology and high-resolution imaging systems to transform how utilities monitor their infrastructure. We specialize in rapidly identifying critical issues including electrical faults, cracked or damaged insulators, vegetation encroachment, and structural defects that could compromise grid performance. Our advanced aerial platforms follow precisely programmed flight paths, capturing detailed visual and thermal data along entire transmission and distribution corridors.

By replacing traditional ground-based inspections with our efficient aerial solutions, utilities can dramatically reduce operational costs associated with field crew deployment and vehicle maintenance. Our technology enables inspection teams to cover more territory in less time, minimizing truck rolls while maximizing asset visibility. This proactive approach to infrastructure monitoring helps utilities maintain superior grid reliability, prevent outages before they occur, and ensure compliance with vegetation management regulations. The result is a safer, more resilient power delivery system that keeps communities connected while optimizing maintenance budgets and resource allocation.

Substation Inspections

Transform your substation maintenance program with our comprehensive aerial inspection services that combine cutting-edge radiometric thermal imaging technology with ultra-high-resolution optical zoom cameras to deliver unprecedented visibility into your critical electrical infrastructure. Our specialized inspection platforms enable thorough evaluations of energized substations from safe distances, eliminating the need for personnel to enter hazardous environments or implement costly service interruptions that impact grid reliability and customer satisfaction.

Our advanced radiometric thermal cameras provide precise, calibrated temperature measurements for every component within the substation, from power transformers and circuit breakers to bus connections and switching equipment. This sophisticated thermal analysis instantly reveals developing problems such as overheating connections caused by increased resistance, failing bushings, deteriorating contacts, imbalanced loads, or inadequate cooling systems. By capturing accurate temperature differentials and thermal patterns, our technology enables predictive maintenance teams to identify and address potential failures weeks or months before they result in catastrophic equipment damage or unplanned outages.

Complementing our thermal capabilities, our high-powered optical zoom cameras deliver crystal-clear visual inspections that detect surface-level defects invisible to the naked eye from ground level. These cameras identify early-stage corrosion on transformer radiators, microscopic cracks in porcelain insulators, contamination buildup, oil staining indicating leaks, SF6 gas emissions, loose hardware, animal damage, and structural fatigue in support frameworks. The ability to conduct these detailed inspections while maintaining safe distances from energized equipment represents a paradigm shift in substation maintenance practices.

By performing regular aerial inspections without de-energizing equipment, utilities maximize operational efficiency while creating comprehensive digital records of asset conditions over time. This data-driven approach supports informed decision-making, optimizes capital planning, ensures regulatory compliance, and ultimately delivers more reliable power to customers while protecting the safety of inspection personnel and maintenance crews.
